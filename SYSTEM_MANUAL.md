# RPAS System Manual
### Research Planning and Analytic Services — Aldersgate College Inc.
*For use as AI Chatbot knowledge base*

---

## What is RPAS?

RPAS (Research Planning and Analytic Services) is the official research support service of Aldersgate College Inc. (ACI). It helps students, researchers, faculty, and groups with data analysis, manuscript review, questionnaire validation, and research consultation. All service requests are tracked online through this system.

---

## Who Can Use RPAS?

There are three types of users in the system:

**Researcher (Student/Faculty)**
A student or faculty member who needs research support. They submit requests, track progress, and communicate with their assigned analyst through the system.

**Data Analyst**
A trained staff member of RPAS who handles the submitted requests. They are assigned by the admin, update the status of requests, and upload results when done.

**Admin**
The RPAS office staff who manages everything — approves new accounts, assigns analysts to requests, monitors all requests, and manages users.

---

## How to Get Started

**Step 1 — Sign In**
Go to the RPAS website and click "Sign in with Google." Use your Aldersgate College email if you are a student or faculty, or your registered email if you are staff.

**Step 2 — Wait for Approval**
After signing in for the first time, your account will be in "Pending" status. The admin will review and approve your account. You will receive a notification once approved.

**Step 3 — Complete Your Profile (Researchers only)**
Before you can submit a request, you must complete your academic profile:
- Sex
- Level (College or Basic Education)
- Department (based on your level)
- Program or Course (based on your department)

You will see a yellow warning in the sidebar if your profile is incomplete. Click "Update now" or "Edit Profile" to fill it in.

**Step 4 — Submit a Request**
Once your profile is complete, click "New Request" to submit.

## What Services Does RPAS Offer?

| Service | What it is |
|---|---|
| **Quantitative Data Analysis** | Statistical analysis of numerical data (SPSS, frequency, regression, correlation, etc.) |
| **Qualitative Data Analysis** | Analysis of interviews, focus groups, narratives, or thematic content |
| **Questionnaire Validation** | Content and construct validity testing of survey instruments |
| **Reliability Test** | Cronbach's Alpha or other reliability measures for your questionnaire |
| **Manuscript Review** | Checking and improving your research paper for grammar, structure, and consistency |
| **Research Consultation** | One-on-one or group consultation about your research design, methodology, or data |

## How to Submit a Request

1. Log in as a Researcher
2. Make sure your profile is complete (Level, Department, Program)
3. Click **"New Request"** on the dashboard
4. Fill in:
   - **Service Type** — choose from the dropdown
   - **Research Title / Topic** — the title of your study
   - **Research Abstract** — 100–150 words describing your study and methods; include 5–8 keywords at the end
   - **Attach Files** (optional) — PDF, DOCX, XLSX, JPG, or PNG, up to 20MB
5. Click **"Submit Request"**

You will receive a notification confirming your submission.

---

## What Happens After You Submit?

Your request goes through the following stages:

| Status | What it means |
|---|---|
| **Submitted** | Your request was received and is waiting for admin review |
| **Under Review** | Admin is reviewing your request and will assign an analyst |
| **In Progress** | An analyst has been assigned and is working on your request |
| **For Revision** | The analyst needs something from you — check messages or notes |
| **Completed** | Your request is done — results have been uploaded |
| **Cancelled** | The request was cancelled by admin or at your request |

You will receive a notification every time your status changes.

## How to Track Your Request

1. Log in as a Researcher
2. On the dashboard, you will see your requests listed with their current status
3. Click on any request to view full details including:
   - Current status and history of all status changes
   - Notes from the analyst or admin
   - Attached files and uploaded results
   - Messages between you and your analyst

---

## How to Message Your Analyst

Once a request is In Progress, you can send messages to your assigned analyst directly within the request detail view. Click on the request, scroll to the Messages section, and type your message.

---

## How to Edit Your Profile

1. Look at the left sidebar
2. Click **"Edit Profile"** (the pencil icon button)
3. You can update:
   - Full Name
   - Contact Number
   - Profile Photo (click the photo to upload a new one)
   - Sex, Level, Department, Program/Course
4. Click **"Save Changes"**

Your name and photo will update immediately in the sidebar.

---

## How to Use Notifications

The bell icon (🔔) at the top right of every page shows your notifications.

- A number badge on the bell means you have unread notifications
- Click the bell to open the notification panel
- Click any notification to go to the relevant request or page
- Click **"×"** on any notification to delete it
- Click **"Mark All as Read"** to clear the unread count

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: I signed in but my account says "Pending." What do I do?**
A: Wait for the admin to approve your account. You will receive a notification by email and inside the system once you are approved. If it has been more than 2 working days, contact the RPAS office.

**Q: I cannot submit a request. The button is blocked.**
A: Your profile is incomplete. You need to fill in your Level, Department, and Program/Course under Edit Profile before you can submit.

**Q: How long does it take to process my request?**
A: Processing time depends on the service type and current workload. Research Consultation can usually be scheduled within a few days. Data analysis services typically take 3–7 working days. You will be notified of any updates.

**Q: Can I submit more than one request at a time?**
A: Yes, you can have multiple active requests at the same time.

**Q: I submitted the wrong service type. Can I cancel?**
A: Contact the RPAS admin through the system. If your request has not yet been assigned to an analyst, it can be cancelled.

**Q: How do I attach my data files?**
A: When submitting a request, click the file upload area (or drag and drop). Accepted formats: PDF, DOCX, XLSX, XLS, JPG, JPEG, PNG. Maximum size is 20MB per file.

**Q: Can I see who my assigned analyst is?**
A: Yes. Once an analyst is assigned, their name will appear on your request detail page.

**Q: My analyst sent me a message but I cannot find it.**
A: Go to your request list, click on the relevant request, and scroll to the Messages section at the bottom of the request detail.

**Q: I uploaded the wrong file. Can I replace it?**
A: Contact your analyst or the admin through the messaging system. They can advise on next steps.

**Q: What is a Research Abstract and why is it required?**
A: The abstract is a short summary (100–150 words) of your research. It helps the analyst understand your study before they start working on it. Include the purpose of your study, the methods you will use, and 5–8 relevant keywords at the end.

**Q: I do not see the notification bell / my dashboard is blank.**
A: Try a hard refresh (Ctrl+Shift+R on Windows, Cmd+Shift+R on Mac). If the problem continues, sign out and sign back in. If still blank, contact the RPAS admin.

## For Admins

**Approving new users:**
Go to Admin Dashboard → Pending Users → click Approve or Reject next to each user.

**Assigning an analyst:**
Go to any submitted request → click "Manage" → select an analyst from the dropdown → save. The analyst will be notified automatically.

**Updating request status:**
Open the Manage dialog for any request → select the new status → add notes if needed → save. The researcher will be notified automatically.

**Managing users:**
Go to Admin Dashboard → Users tab → view all users by role and status.

---

## For Analysts

**Viewing your assigned requests:**
Your dashboard shows all requests assigned to you. Each card shows the researcher name, service type, and current status.

**Updating a request:**
Click on any request → update the status (e.g., In Progress, For Revision, Completed) → add notes for the researcher.

**Uploading results:**
When a request is ready, upload the results file through the request detail page. The researcher will be notified.

**Messaging a researcher:**
Open the request → scroll to Messages → type your message and send.
